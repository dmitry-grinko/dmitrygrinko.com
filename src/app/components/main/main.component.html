Notes on machine learning, bioinformatics, and computational biology from the perspective of a software engineer

Hello, I'm Dmitry Grinko.

This blog documents my journey into machine learning and bioinformatics from the perspective of a software engineer who enjoys understanding how complex systems work.

I use this space as a personal learning notebook — a place to explore ideas, break down difficult concepts, and share what I learn along the way.

My background

My path into technology was not straightforward.

I graduated from Brest State Technical University (Belarus) with a degree in Civil Engineering (2005–2010). After graduation, I spent four years working as a design engineer in architectural and construction firms in Belarus, mainly on residential projects.

Over time I found myself increasingly interested in technology and software. In 2015, I began learning programming, starting with web and mobile development before gradually moving toward backend engineering and cloud architecture.

In 2018, my family and I immigrated to the United States.

Today I work at Portland General Electric as an Application Developer, where I focus on backend systems and API integrations running in AWS.

Why this blog exists

Outside of work I spend much of my time studying:

  • Machine Learning
  • Bioinformatics
  • Computational Biology
  • Data-driven approaches to biological systems

These fields are fascinating because they sit at the intersection of biology, mathematics, and software engineering.

However, much of the material in this area is either highly academic or difficult for engineers to approach. My goal is to explain these topics in a way that is clear, practical, and accessible for developers.

What you'll find here

On this blog I write about:

  • biology fundamentals explained for engineers
  • machine learning applied to biological data
  • genomics and protein modeling basics
  • small ML experiments and projects
  • notes from papers and research I study
  • my personal learning process

I try to explain ideas as simply as possible — the same way I would explain them to another developer.

A note on perspective

This blog is not written from the perspective of a researcher or professor.

It's written from the perspective of someone who is learning, experimenting, and building understanding step by step. You'll see ideas evolving over time, mistakes, corrections, and attempts to understand complex topics from first principles.

If you're reading this

This blog may be useful if you are:

  • a software engineer curious about machine learning
  • a developer exploring bioinformatics
  • someone interested in how biology and computation intersect

If that sounds like you — welcome.
